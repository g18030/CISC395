Montréal feels lively, walkable, and a little European, with old stone streets in the historic core, busy cafes, street art, and a strong student energy. It is a great city for wandering, eating well on a budget, and switching between French and English as you explore. The best season is late spring through early fall, especially from May to October, when the weather is pleasant, patios are open, and festivals fill the city. In winter, the city can still be fun, but the cold is serious.

Try poutine, ideally from a local casse-croûte, for the classic fries, cheese curds, and gravy combination. Grab a Montréal-style bagel from St-Viateur or Fairmount, and sample smoked meat on rye for another local favorite. If you have a sweet tooth, a maple treat is also worth it.

For activities, walk through Old Montréal, climb or picnic on Mount Royal, and explore the Plateau for murals and neighborhood cafes. Student travelers should buy a transit pass if staying more than a day or two, since it saves money and time. Also, book popular food spots and hostel beds early during festival season, because prices rise fast.
